4237 The Devourer of Dreams
The title “The Devourer of Dreams” finds its origin in the teddy bear. The figure retains the appearance of an affective object, constructed to provide stability and emotional continuity. Its form recalls protection, memory, childhood. This image generates a system of meanings that accompanies the gaze and produces a sense of orientation.
The eyes introduce a fracture. The dark surface does not return a readable depth. The gaze that approaches encounters an opaque density that interrupts the process of recognition. At that point, the meaning constructed by the object loses consistency. The teddy bear, in its entirety, generates the illusion and consumes it precisely at the point where the gaze concentrates. The “devouring” coincides with this passage: the construction of meaning and its collapse occur within the same element.

Below, the figures in motion move through space following a regular continuity. The flow proceeds through bodies that register the effects of events. Each passage connects to the previous one and prepares the next. Experience accumulates without creating a point of arrival.
The title then becomes clear as the description of a mechanism. Dreams emerge as constructions necessary to perception. The same structure that generates them also contains the point at which they are consumed. The teddy bear becomes the locus of this dynamic: it produces meaning and reabsorbs it, leaving the gaze in a more exposed and direct condition.
