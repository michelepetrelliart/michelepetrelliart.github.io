4279 The Usurper
This balcony transforms into a theater of ontological usurpation. The skull on the ledge is the seal of a broken pact: contemporary man, terrified by biological limits and the awareness of an existence perceived as a failure, has ceased to accept his own finitude. In an attempt to become eternal, to emulate the myth of technological immortality and the persistence of the self, the adult has lost his role as a shield. The grandfather is no longer offering protection to the sprout; he is consuming it. The child, in the innocence of his gesture, does not realize he has become a mere aesthetic prop for an old age that refuses to face its sunset. It is the face of radical egoism: the generation that failed to give meaning to its own time now seeks to plunder the time of others, absorbing the energy and financial resources of the future to fuel a present that refuses to step aside. In this embrace, the child is no longer the staff of old age, but a foothold for a shipwrecked man who, instead of letting go of the sea, decides to drag down with him those who have just arrived at the shore. The debt—economic, existential, moral—that the old man leaves as an inheritance to the little one is the only true certainty of this painting. Tenderness, in this light, is merely the final bait of a predator who has traded spiritual legacy for the exploitation of the living. There is a direct link between this desire for the 'eternal'—a reference to Musk and success at all costs—and the visual image of the child being used as a tool. It is an indictment of the 'consumption' of the new generations, no longer viewed as a future to be protected, but as a resource to be drained.
