4221 No Siege
The dualistic eye perceives an assault: three predators, one prey, a struggle for survival. Within Radical Not-Dual Determinism (RNDD), this interpretation remains a perspectival error. A siege stays absent because separate entities in conflict do not exist. What appears as violence represents the inevitable convergence of predetermined forces collapsing into a single point of manifestation. The three tigers embody an ancient hunger. This persecution manifests as an external force, fueled by the desire to consume the flesh and the very essence of the subject. In RNDD, this "great hunger" stands apart from the will of the three figures or the misfortune of the individual. Their voracity and my resistance exist as the same substance expressing itself through opposing vectors. The process remains the only reality: the naked, raw occurrence of a tension incapable of being other than what it is.
To assert No Siege denies the role of the victim. Under determinism, persecution lacks moral charge, becoming pure existential mechanics. The tigers seek to consume every fiber; however, their target belongs to the same deterministic fabric that moves them. The lacerated flesh and the striking claw function as parts of the same equation.
This work stands as the mapping of a condition where the drive for consumption and the resistance of form cancel each other out through non-duality. The center remains intact due to necessity; its persistence equals the hunger attempting to destroy it. In this event, the predator equals the prey. The silence of determinism provides the only possible response to the sound of the bites.
